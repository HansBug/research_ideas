Abstract —Safety testing is a key method to ensure software
quality. But the quality of the test depends on the level of the
test engineers. The method of generating safety test cases based
on state diagrams often perform poorly due to lack of personnel
experience. Meanwhile, the manual construction of test cases
constrains the improvement of safety testing efficiency. This arti-
cle utilizes LLMs to achieve automated extension and testing of
safety state diagrams. The main contributions include extracting
safety properties from aviation standards and extending the basic
state diagram with LLMs to enable safety testing capabilities. In
addition, a genetic algorithm combining edge first depth traversal
and LLMs optimization was proposed to improve the efficiency of
test case generation. Compared to classical genetic algorithms, the
average execution round achieved a 90% reduction. Our LLMs
optimization strategy can also enhance other improved genetic
algorithms, further improving the efficiency of safety testing.
Index Terms—safety testing, LLMs, safety state diagrams, test
case generation
I. I NTRODUCTION
Software safety can detect hidden safety vulnerabilities
within the system and has received increasing attention,
especially in the aerospace industry. The development and
certification of avionics systems follow strict standards, such
as DO-178C [1] and SAE ARP 4754A [2], ensuring safety
and reliability under all conditions. However, these standards
lack detailed safety assessments for software and hardware,
requiring testers to independently understand how to test safety
[3]. The quality of software safety testing heavily relies on the
historical experience of testers, often resulting in incomplete
safety analysis perspectives. So, engineers with rich experience
in safety testing are the most important part of the entire testing
work [4].
In recent years, the Model Based Safety Analysis (MBSA)
method [5], [6] has been widely applied in the safety analysisand verification of aviation and other safety critical systems
[7]. The software state diagram expresses the basic functions
of the model. In order to conduct safety analysis systemati-
cally, testers need to manually expand the state diagram. The
basis for expansion is the common safety properties of the
tested software. Due to the varying levels of proficiency among
testers, there may be omissions or errors in the state diagram
extension, resulting in poor testing quality. And the efficiency
of safety testing is difficult to effectively improve. Large
Language Models (LLMs) play a revolutionary role in the field
of artificial intelligence (AI) [8], [9]. By training on large-scale
textual data, LLMs master complex patterns and dependencies
in language, demonstrating excellent reasoning and problem-
solving abilities [10]. Among these advanced LLMs, models
such as ChatGPT, LLaMA, and QWEN [11] have billions
of parameters. These models are widely used in content
generation and software testing. So we consider using LLMs
to automate the state graph for comprehensive extension. This
approach can avoid omissions in safety testing caused by
inexperienced personnel and improve testing efficiency.
After completing the safety state diagram extension, it is
necessary to automatically generate corresponding test cases.
Model based test case generation includes two parts: test path
generation and input data inference. Test path are subject to
varying levels of traversal complexity, and the input data range
of a system is typically large [19], making efficient inference
of input data challenging. Therefore, finding a suitable set of
input data to trigger the test sequence is a challenging task.
Based on the widespread application of search algorithms in
various fields, the use of genetic algorithms [18] for input
data inference is widely applied. But only using genetic
algorithms for data inference did not consider the possible data
dependencies between transitions [20]. Population size and

In this paper, we utilize LLMs to automatically extend

Simultaneously utilizing LLMs to optimize the hyperparam-

contributions of this paper are:
Extraction of safety criterion: We extracted specific safety
requirements related to state diagram safety extensions from
key standards DO-178C and ARP 4754A in the aviation field,

Safety state diagram extension based on large language

II. S AFETY STATE DIAGRAM EXTENSION AND TESTCASE
GENERATION
This section first introduces the theme framework for
extending safety state diagrams and generating test cases.
Then, it describes an extension method that combines safety
criterion with state diagrams. Finally, it outlines the specific
implementation method for generating test cases based on
these extended state diagrams.
A. Framework Overview
Figure 1 delineates the systematic process of extracting
safety criterion, extending safety state diagrams, and gener-
ating test cases, structured into five primary steps. In Step
1, safety criteria are extracted from well-established safety
criterion such as DO-178C and APR 4754A. This foundational
step ensures that the subsequent processes are rooted in
recognized and rigorous safety protocols.In Step 2, the extracted safety criteria and state diagram
XML files are utilized as inputs to a LLMs. Given the
unique nature of LLMs, it is crucial to design precise input
instructions (prompts) to guide the model effectively. The
LLMs processes these inputs, and in Step 3, produces an
extended state diagram that incorporates detailed safety status
information, thereby enhancing the original state diagram with
additional safety insights.
Step 4 involves performing an edge-first depth traversal on
the safety-enhanced state diagram generated in Step 3. This
traversal method is employed to generate a comprehensive test
path specific to the safety state diagram. Finally, in Step 5,
a genetic algorithm is used to deduce the input data for the
test paths derived in Step 4, resulting in the generation of
robust test cases. Notably, during this step, the hyper-parameter
settings and mutation algorithms of the genetic algorithm are
optimized using the LLMs.

follows the expected logical flow. At the same time, using

D. Test Case Generation
In the testing process based on state diagrams, Depth First
Search (DFS) usually consists of two key steps: generating test
paths and deducing test data. The edge first depth traversal
strategy, compared to node first depth traversal, can more
comprehensively cover all transitions in the state graph and
better maintain the orderliness between states.
The edge first depth first traversal algorithm recursively
traverses all nodes and edges in the graph, recording all
independent paths and edges traversed. The algorithm starts
from the starting node and gradually delves into each unvisited
edge until all edges are accessed. During the traversal process,
the current path and visited edges are dynamically updated,
and the comprehensiveness and integrity of the traversal are
ensured through a backtracking mechanism. This method
ensures that all transitions and state sequences in the graph
are fully considered and covered.
Genetic algorithm is an optimization algorithm based on
natural selection and genetic mechanisms. Its steps include
initializing hyper-parameters, generating initial population,
calculating individual fitness, individual mutation, individual
crossover, selecting individuals, and determining whether in-
dividuals meet the requirements. In this section, we introduce
LLMs to guide the hyperparameter generation and individual
mutation process of genetic algorithms The specific implemen-
tation details are as follows:
Hyper-parameter Generation: Using a large model to
generate hyper-parameters (POPULATIONIZE, CROSSOV-
RATE, MUTATION-RATE, MAXGENERATES) for genetic
algorithms, by integrating state diagrams and test path in-
formation, the large model can make more informed hyper-
parameter setting decisions in the context of specific problems.
In prompt design, we embed the state diagram and test path
information into the prompt prompt words and input them
together to the large model. Through this method, the large
model can fully understand the specific context of the cur-
rent problem, thereby generating more accurate and effective
hyper-parameter configurations. This not only improves the
overall performance of genetic algorithms, but also signif-
icantly reduces the workload of manual parameter tuning.
Meanwhile, this prompt design that combines state diagrams
and test path information ensures that the large model can
comprehensively consider the complexity and diversity of testcases when generating hyper-parameters, thereby improving
the adaptability and robustness of the algorithm in practical
applications.The prompt design is shown in Figure 5.
Population: Each individual in the population consists of N
variable values, where Nrepresents the number of variables in
the transition trigger conditions in the test path, and, N∈N.
Population = [V1, V2, V3, . . . , V N] (1)
Each Vi,i∈N, represents the specific value of the i-th
variable. During population initialization, the variable values
are randomly generated according to their types, which include
integer (int) variables, boolean variables, and float variables.
Specifically, the population initialization process is as follows:
1.Integer variables (int): These variables are randomly
generated within a given integer range. If a variable Viis of
type integer, its value is randomly selected between the preset
minimum and maximum values.
2.Boolean variables (boolean): These variables represent
binary values of true or false. During initialization, each
boolean variable Viis equally likely to be set to true or false.
3.Float variables (float): These variables are randomly
generated within a given floating-point range. If a variable
Viis of type float, its value is randomly selected within
the continuous interval between the preset minimum and
maximum values.
By randomly generating values for each variable type, the
diversity of the initial population can be ensured. This diversity
is crucial for the effectiveness of optimization algorithms or
evolutionary algorithms, as it provides a rich initial solution
space, thereby increasing the likelihood of finding the global
optimum.

Selection: Using sorting selection method to select indi-
viduals, first, calculate the fitness value of each individual in
the population. The fitness function evaluates an individual’s
performance in problem-solving based on their characteristics.
Sort individuals in the population from high to low based on
their fitness values, with individuals with high fitness values
ranking first and those with low fitness values ranking last.
This step ensures that individuals with high fitness are at the
top of the ranking. Each individual is assigned a selection
probability based on their position in the ranking. Individuals
with higher rankings (i.e. higher fitness values) have a higher
probability of selection. Accumulate the selection probabilities
assigned to each individual to form a cumulative probability
distribution, which can be obtained by gradually adding up the
selection probabilities. Generate a random number between0 and 1, and select an individual based on the cumulative
probability distribution.
Individual Mutation: Individual mutations in genetic al-

we use LLMs instead of random mutation of individuals

each individual to develop towards a higher fitness direction,
thereby generating more excellent individuals for screening
in genetic algorithms. Due to the consideration of contextual

IV. RELATED WORK
This section reviews the most pertinent literature and pre-
vious research efforts. In terms of generating test paths based
on state diagrams, A UML state diagram based automated
statistical test case generation technique has been proposed
to improve the efficiency and coverage of object-oriented
software testing. By using coverage conversion as the main
testing standard and combining it with Rational Software Cor-
poration’s Rose RealTime tool, automatic generation of test
inputs and expected outputs has been achieved. The application
of this method in the case study of avionics systems has
verified its effectiveness and feasibility in practical complex
system testing [16]. Utilizes the control and data flow logic in
the state diagram to automatically traverse the state machine
diagram and select conditional predicates on the transfor-
mation, then transform these predicates and apply function
minimization techniques to generate test cases. This method
is capable of generating test cases that meet the transition path
coverage criteria, suitable for testing state dependent behaviorsat the class and cluster levels, and improving test coverage
through boundary testing and predicate coverage. Ultimately,
automated test case generation is achieved through a prototype
tool called UTG [27]. In the context of genetic algorithms,
In related research fields, other researchers have proposed
various improvement strategies for genetic algorithms to over-
come the limitations of traditional methods. These include
the introduction of adaptive crossover and mutation operators,
optimization of selection and crossover operators based on
complex system theory, fusion of negative selection strategies,
implementation of algorithm parallelization, and multi-path
coverage test case generation methods for complex systems.
These improvements significantly enhance the global search
capability, efficiency, and path coverage of the algorithm,
bringing significant progress to the field of software testing
automation [17]- [21]. LLMs have been effectively used for
various coding tasks, including code and unit test generation,
presenting unique challenges and opportunities in software
testing due to its distinct focus on diverse test inputs and cov-
erage, resulting in innovative practices leveraging LLMs [22]-
[25]. In terms of parameter tuning guided by LLMs, LLAMBO
enhances Bayesian optimization by integrating LLMs and
solving efficient hyper-parameter tuning of Bayesian optimiza-
tion in data scarcity situations by using LLMs and designing
corresponding prompt prompts [26].

can automatically extend four types of safety properties.
Regarding the testing path coverage of genetic algorithm, the
optimized genetic algorithm reduces the maximum number of
iterations by about 90%. Regarding various improved genetic
algorithms proposed in recent years, the average number of
iterations has been reduced by about 77.32%, with the highest
reduction being about 94.31%. The results indicate that LLM
effectively guides the model optimization process, reduces the
model solving process, and improves the efficiency of test case
generation.
Based on the identified issues in the current study, the
following future work is proposed to address these limitations
and enhance the overall approach:
Enrichment of Safety Criteria Extraction: The current
safety criteria encompass four main categories and twenty-
five sub-items. Future work should focus on expanding these
criteria and categories to include additional safety aspects,
such as control logic, processing logic, and transfer processes.
Achieving this expansion will require ongoing review and
integration of new criteria and best practices from a variety of
safety domains. By doing so, we can ensure that the criteria
remain comprehensive and relevant, effectively addressing
emerging safety challenges and technological advancements.
Context-Aware Path Generation: The current method
of test path generation does not account for the contextual
relationships between different transitions. This oversight can
lead to paths with conflicting or incomplete execution condi-
tions. Future work should focus on developing context-aware
path generation techniques that consider the dependencies and
shared variables between different transitions.
In summary, we utilize the automated extension of state
diagrams and the automatic generation of test cases to en-
hance the safety testing efficiency of testing engineers. Safety
testing can be automated and efficiently carried out, reducing
reliance on manual operations. The proposed future work aims
to address the limitations identified in current research by
enriching safety standards, developing robust sample libraries,
and incorporating context aware path generation techniques.
