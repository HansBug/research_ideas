Abstract  
Traffic congestion at road intersections is a prevalent issue in urban areas, posing significant challenges. The current traffic 
light control system is insufficient in effectively managing congestion due to limitations in handling all necessary controls  
within its structure. A few related problems with the existing design of the tr affic control system include fixed time intervals 
and the absence of provisions for emergency vehicles. To address these concerns, a proposed solution incorporates the 
application of a sensor built into the traffic control system and programmable logic con trol (PLC). The sensor is attached 
to monitor the congestion level in each lane, quickly assessing the number of vehicles in less than 2 seconds. Based on this 
information, a signal is sent to activate the Green light for the lane with the highest vehicle count, while signaling Red for 
the other lanes. With the existing traffic system, emergency vehicles such as police vans, ambulances have difficulties at 
traffic light junctions. To overcome these challenges, a smart traffic controlling system is proposed.  This system effectively 
manages the presence of emergency vehicles by turning all signals red, except for one, when an emergency vehicle 
approaches. By doing so, the system being proposed operates more efficiently than the existing traffic control system,  
resulting in reduced waiting times, improved emergency mode operations, and a decrease in the risk of accidents  
. 
Keywords:  Programmable Logic Cont rol (PLC), Traffic congestion, S ensor, Traffic light control, Emergency 
vehicles.  
1. INTRODUCTION  
Traffic signals, also known as traffic lights, are 
devices strategically positioned at road 
intersections and pedestrian crossings to 
regulate flow of traffic and avoid conflicts in 
movement of vehicles [1]. The Smart Traffic 
Controller system, also referr ed to as advanced 
traffic lights or intelligent traffic lights [2], 
utilizes a combination of existing technology 
and artificial intelligence to create lights that can 
make independent decisions. Unlike traditional 
traffic light systems, this innovative sy stem 
aims to address the limitations of conventional 
traffic control methods. In busy junctions, traffic 
lights have been widely used as a convenient means of managing traffic [3]. However, their 
effectiveness diminishes when one lane 
becomes more congeste d than the others [4]. 
This imbalance leads to increased congestion 
and potential accidents, resulting in loss of life, 
time, and energy [5]. Many existing traffic light 
systems operate based on predefined timing 
intervals, which may not adequately respond  to 
the real -time presence or absence of vehicles 
[6]. In contrast, an intelligent traffic light system 
employs various sensing techniques to detect 
traffic and adjust signal timings accordingly [7].  
By using sensors to detect vehicle presence and 
calcula te density, the smart traffic system can 
make informed decisions to prioritize specific

 lanes [8]. Programmable Logic Controller

This research paper proposes the use of sensors

relay this information to the programmable logic

current traffic system relies on a countdown 
mechanism of 120 seconds befor e triggering on 
the green LED for vehicle movement.  The 
primary objective of studying the impact of the 
Smart Traffic Controller Signal Simulator is to 
reduce the time for which cars wait on each lane, 
enhance road safety, minimize accidents, and 
maximize the throughput of vehicles crossing an 
intersection within a given timeframe. 
Achieving this will result in reduced exhaust gas 
emissions and improve life satisfaction of city 
citizens [12] . Many scientists have conducted 
research that can lessen the negat ive 
consequences of traffic congestion. Pandey et al. 
developed a plan for distributing the times for 
traffic lights after gathering information about 
the intersection's traffic density through the use  of image and video analysis. The suggested 
approach w as effective for traffic control [13].  
An effectively constructed fuzzy logic based on 
Wireless Sensor Network was proposed by Zou 
et al. to control traffic lights for a single 
junction. [14]. In accordance with this 
paradigm, single -axis magneto sensors t hat 
were positioned alongside roads were used to 
track the movement of traffic. The fuzzy 
algorithm was used to modify the passing time 
for cars based on the number of available 
vehicles. Their study found that for the 
simulation period, real -time control was 
attained with an average waiting time reduction 
of roughly 22.7% when compared to the 
conventional fixed cycle method.  Siyal and 
Fathy applied edge detection to develop a 
process that improves traffic flow at the 
intersection. In this process, edge det ection was 
used to identify vehicles and estimate their 
movement [15]

Software Simulation  
Once the hardware setup is complete, a ladder 
logic code and downloaded into the Micrologix 
1500 PLC using the code editor. Addit ionally, 
the countdown timer, which interfaces with the 
traffic system through a seven -segment display, 
is controlled by a PIC microcontroller. The PIC 
microcontroller is programmed using Flowcode 
Version 5 for PICmicros. The entire system is 
then simulate d using Logix Pro and Flowcode 
V5 for PIC microcontroller.  
 
Implementation  
The initial step in implementing the simulator 
involves installing the necessary hardware 
components. The Micrologix 1500 PLC, 
indicator LEDs and inductive sensors must be 
correctl y wired and connected. Next, the written 
codes are downloaded to the Micrologix 1500 
PLC. For each lane, two inductive sensors are 
installed, and the program will assess the sensor 
conditions to determine if they have been triggered or not. The ladder logi c program 
utilizes the total number of triggered sensors to 
determine the appropriate timing for triggering 
ON the green LED. Once the green LED timing 
is completed, the yellow LED will then 
illuminate for 5 seconds, followed by the red 
LED. The traffic si gnal will then either wait or 
yield to vehicles on the other lanes based on the 
sensor conditions in those respective lanes. In 
the event that none of the lanes experience 
significant traffic congestion, the system will 
operate according to the predefined timer that 
has been programmed into the PLC.  
 
3. RESULT AND DISCUSION  
The traffic signal operation initiates with the red 
LEDs triggered ON for 1 minute on lane 1 and 
lane 3, while the green LED is triggered ON on 
lane 2 and lane 4. After 45 seconds from t he start 
of the operation, the yellow indicator LED on 
lane 2 and lane 4 starts blinking. Subsequently, 
the operation proceeds with the red LEDs being

 triggered ON for 1 minute on lane 2 and lane 4, 
and the green LED is ON on Lane 1 and lane 3. 
Again, afte r 45 seconds from the start of the 
operation, the yellow indicator LEDs on lane 1 
and lane 3 starts blinking. This sequence 
continues in a repeated manner unless any of the 
roads' sensors are overrides the timing sequence 
as a result of congestion.  
 
It should be noted that green LED on any 
congested lane will be triggered ON except if 
any of the following situations occur;  
  
i. All the lanes are congested.  
ii. Lane 1 and lane 2 or lane 4 are congested  
iii. Lane 3 and lane 2 or lane 4 are 
congested.  
 
In the event that the above cases occur, then the 
timing sequence will prevail over the sensor 
readings.  
 
To inform drivers about the remaining waiting 
time in descending order, the seven -segment 
display is utilized. Inductive sensors are 
employed to detect t he presence of vehicles in 
all four directions. When a vehicle blocks a 
sensor at a certain distance, the sensor is 
triggered, relaying the information to the Micrologix 1500 PLC that there is a vehicle in 
the corresponding lane. If no vehicles are 
present  on any of the lanes, the LEDs will 
transition from green to yellow within 2 
seconds, and from yellow to red in an additional 
2 seconds. This cycle continues sequentially, 
starting from lane 1, followed by lane 2, lane 3, 
and finally lane 4. If there are t wo vehicles on 
lane 1, the time taken for the green LED to 
change to yellow is 16 seconds, and the same 
principle applies to the other lanes.

5. CONCLUTION  
The impact of the Smart Traffic Controller 
system on the existing Traffic Controller System 
cannot be overstated. The Smart Traffic 
Controller system efficiently manages traffic 
flow in up to 4 -phase traffic systems, employing 
optimization strategies to enhance traffic flow at 
network junctions. It demonstrates measurable 
improvements in traffic flow, reduces 
environmental pollution resulting from traff ic, 
and significantly enhances road safety by 
reducing accidents.  The implementation of 
smart traffic lights brings numerous advantages. 
Optimal waiting times for vehicles at 
intersections lead to a reduction in carbon 
monoxide emissions from vehicles, pos itively 
contributing to the environment and mitigating 
the greenhouse effect. Moreover, the smart 
traffic system saves motorists' time and 
alleviates their frustration by minimizing 
congestion at intersections, thus expediting their 
journeys. An additional  benefit is the absence of 
interference among the sensor rays, resulting in the elimination of redundant signal triggering.  
Overall, the Smart Traffic Controller system 
revolutionizes traffic management, yielding 
enhanced efficiency, reduced environmental 
impact, and improved safety for all road users.
