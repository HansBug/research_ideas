Abstract  
The three -pulse system of automatic control of the water level in the boiler drum received maximum 
distribution in the energy sector, as well as its modifications, which have the following dis advantages : 
the presence of static control errors at the end of the transition process in the development of internal 
disturbances and disturbances of superheated steam flow; large level deviations with sharp and significant 
changes in the load, which oft en leads to unloading or shutdown of the boiler or power unit. To eliminate 
these drawbacks, invariant systems for automatic regulation of the power supply of the drum boiler were 
previously developed. In work the system of automatic control of technologic al parameters of water level 
in a drum of steam boilers is considered. The description of control systems and a detailed description of 
the electrical power systems of the control system are given. Various methods of tuning the PI controller 
of a typical t hree-pulse automatic power control system are considered. The application of the method of 
structural -parametric optimization and the theory of invariance makes it possible by changing the 
structure of the system and optimizing the dynamic adjustment of th e automatic power control system to 
significantly improve the quality of maintaining the water level in the boiler drum. Invariant automatic 
control systems eliminate static control errors under all influences and can significantly improve the 
quality of m aintaining the level under variable conditions. The methods are proposed to significantly 
improve the quality of water level control in the boiler drum, eliminating the shortcomings of the typical 
automatic control system. The results of the work can be us ed in the design of power control systems of 
steam drum boilers.  The results obtained can be used in the development of adaptive control systems for 
other thermal power  facilities.  
Keywords : automatic control of power supply, PI regulator, Laplace  transfor ms, transient, regulator coefficients, 
water level, drum steam boiler.

I. INTRODUCTION  
Automation of power su pply of drum boilers 
provides automatic control of water supply under 
normal flow of operating modes of the boiler, and 
in the modes of starting and stopping the boiler 
unit. In turn, normal operating modes can occur 
at a constant and variable (sliding) pr essure of 
fresh steam.  
As an indicator of compliance of the material 
balance between steam and water – the flow of 
fresh steam and feed water serves the level of the 
boiler drum. The deviation of the water level in 
the drum from the average value character izes the 
presence of unbalance between the flow of feed 
water and steam flow. It (deviation) also occurs 
due to changes in the steam content of steam in 
the steam -water mixture of lifting pipes due to 
fluctuations in the steam pressure in the boiler 
drum o r changes in the thermal perception of the 
evaporating heating surfaces.  
Thus, with an increase in steam consumption 
at the first moment after the disturbance, the 
water level in the drum increases as a result of a 
sharp decrease in steam pressure, which i n turn 
leads to an increase in the steam content in the 
lifting pipes of the circulation circuit and an 
increase in the level. This phenomenon is called 
level swelling.  
When changing the load of the boiler and, as a 
consequence, changing its steam capacity , the 
average water level should be maintained 
constant. The maximum permissible deviation of 
the water level in the drum is +100 mm from the 
average value set by the manufacturer. In which 
case, the average level does not necessarily have 
to coincide with  the geometric axis of the drum. 
Lowering the level below the visible part of the 
water -measuring glass installed on the drum of 
the boiler unit is considered a ―loss‖ of water, 
and exceeding its upper visible part is referred to 
as ―overfilling‖. The dist ance between these 
critical marks is 400 mm.   
Lowering the level below the connection point 
of the downpipes of the ci rculation circuit can 
lead to power failure and water cooling in the 
lifting pipes, a violation of their strength in the 
areas of docking with the drum body, and in the 
most severe cases, a burnout.  
II.  METHODS  AND MATERIALS  
An e xcessive increase in the level can lead to 
the deterioration of the action within  the drum separation devices, the salt drift of the 
superheater as well as the wate r particles being 
displaced into the turbine, which can cause 
severe mechanical damage to the blades of its 
rotor. The supply of water to the drum is carried 
out through one  line – and in some rare cases, 
two lines – of feed water pipelines , and in the 
case of the latter, one of them  serves as a backup . 
The s cheme of automatic control of the power 
supply of the boiler unit  is as follows : The 
principle of combined perturbation control is 
implemented in the automatic control system 
(ACS) of the boiler wate r supply – when 
changing the flow of steam or feed water and 
deviation – when changing the water level in the 
boiler drum.  
The power regulator should ensure the 
constancy of the average water level regardless of 
the boiler load and disturbance ( Figure  1).

When the load on the boiler is completely

The amount of steam passing through these

the drum in accordance with the uneven level of 
the regulator. Therefore, it is necessary to choose 
the minimum possible amount of unevenness in 
terms of level, providing acceptable dynamic 
quality of ACS power.  
With a sliding (variable) initial steam 
pressure before the turbine for the boiler working

working fluid throughout the steam -water path, 
and the pressure is reduced by reducing the 
rotation speed of the feed pump, this reduces the

number of revolutions of the feed pump (11) 
connected by the hydraulic coupling (12) with the 
electric motor (13) is achieved by the action of 
the performance regulator (9) by the pressure 
drop signal on the feed valve (10) from the 
diphmanometer (14).  
The maximum permissible d eviations of the 
water level in the boiler drum of a particular type 
are set by the manufacturer. Therefore, 
maintaining a given water level in the boiler 
drum is an important technological parameter, on 
which also depends the reliability, safety and 
efficiency of the boiler equipment of the power 
plant. This requirement is an important and 
urgent task in their operation. Steam drum boiler 
units and three -pulse ACS are widely used in 
power engineering, as well as their modifications  
[1].  
A three -pulse ACS regulator ensures good 
testing of jump tasks but does not ensure 
qualitative testing of internal and extreme 
external disturbances (the presence of a static 
error of regulation). This property  of the regulator will reduce the efficiency, reliability , 
and d urability of the boiler, making  it relevant to 
modernize the regulator . The dynamics of the 
water level in the boiler drum when disturbed by 
the flow of fed water is characterized by a lack of 
self-leveling, as well as the phenomenon of 
―level swelling‖ wh en disturbed by the flow of 
superheated steam [2]. 
In a typical three -pulse ACS water supply of 
a steam generator (boiler), which is most 
common ly used in thermal power plants [1 -3], a 
proportional -integral regulator is covered by 
feedback on the flow rate of fed water. The 
introduction of this pulse gives the regulator the 
function of a water flow stabilizer, eliminating 
the dependence of the level on the fluctuations in 
the press ure drop on the valve. Therefore , the 
three -pulse regulator is set so that the same 
change in the consumption of steam and water 
cause s the same movement of the rod for 
regulating the filling valve. With this setting, a 
chang e in the boiler load leads to a  corresponding 
change in water flow, and the ACS goes into a 
new state of equilibrium without a significant 
change in the level. The pulse level is corrective, 
eliminating the inaccuracies of the settings and 
inconsistencies that arise in the process of 
regulation, which is especially important for 
regulating object s without self -leveling [4]. 
The simplest static controller (P -controller) 
has a leading angle equal to
pi , which  
enables  it to provide a minimum dynamic error of 
the adjustable parameter in transient modes for 
objects without self -leveling. The simplest ideal 
astatic regulator (I -regulator) has a small lead 
angle equal to  
2pi , which  is not suitable 
for objects without self -leveling, since the closed 
system becomes neutral or unstable . The advance 
angle of the real integrating regulator occupies an 
intermediate position between these two 
regulators, but the dynamic control error is 
greater than that of the static one. Therefore, in a 
three -pulse ACS of the water level in the boiler 
drum, to increase the stability of the regulation, a 
PI regulator with a rigid feedback is used. In this 
case, the ACS consists of two circuits: internal, 
including a regulator and a rigid feedback device, 
and external, including the object of regulation 
and an equivalent regulator, consisting of a rigid 
feedback device and a regulator [3].

improvement in the quality of regulation i n a

1. A method has bee n developed for calculating

processes showed that when calculating the 
numerical value of the coefficient

618.2 ,

• parallel to the real inertial section of the object

which allows the isolation of an 
equivalent external disturbance without 
measurin g it.

the automatic power control system with the

significant advantage of the latter.
